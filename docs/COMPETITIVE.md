# SAM — Competitive Honesty

*Internal. Where SAM genuinely wins, and where it doesn't — so the pitch is credible, not inflated. A
pitch that admits weaknesses is more persuasive than one that doesn't.*

## The landscape

| | Private/local | Free/offline | Takes real actions | Learns you (on-device) | Ecosystem |
|---|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| **SAM** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ (packs) |
| ChatGPT desktop | ❌ (cloud) | ❌ | partial (limited) | ❌ (their cloud) | ⚠️ (GPTs) |
| Cursor | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ (code only) | ⚠️ (repo, cloud) | ⚠️ |
| Ollama + WebUI | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ (chat only) | ❌ | ❌ |
| Jan | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ (chat only) | ❌ | ⚠️ |

## Where SAM genuinely beats each

- **vs ChatGPT desktop:** privacy + offline + free + it takes real system actions and learns you locally.
  ChatGPT is a better raw model and more polished, but everything you say goes to their cloud.
- **vs Cursor:** Cursor is superb *in the editor*; SAM acts across the *whole computer* (files, email,
  shell, overlay-anywhere) and is private/free. Cursor wins decisively for pure coding.
- **vs Ollama + Open WebUI / Jan:** those give you a private local *chat*; SAM gives you a private local
  *doer* — 170+ tools, workflows, an on-device index that cites your files, an ⌥Space overlay. They're
  the closest on privacy; SAM's edge is that it does things, not just talks.

## Where SAM honestly does NOT win (yet)

- **Raw model quality** on the free/local tier is below GPT-5/Claude on the hardest tasks. SAM's answer is
  the cascade router (use the cheapest brain that's good enough) + bring-your-own premium key — but a
  frontier-model user on a specific hard task may still prefer the frontier product.
- **Polish & scale.** ChatGPT/Cursor have huge teams and years of UX refinement. SAM is (today) essentially
  a solo project — rougher edges, fewer integrations, a young ecosystem.
- **Mobile.** SAM is desktop-first with phone *access* via scoped tokens; it's not a native mobile app.
- **Brand & trust-at-scale.** Nobody's heard of SAM yet. Zero users. That's the entire challenge.

## The honest strategic read

SAM does not win by being a better ChatGPT. It wins by being the **only** option for a specific, growing
audience: people who want an AI that *does real work* but refuse to send their life to a cloud. For that
person, every alternative fails at least one non-negotiable. SAM fails none. That's a narrow beachhead —
but a real, defensible, uncrowded one, and it's exactly the audience that evangelizes (privacy people are
loud). Win them first; the model quality gap closes on its own as open models improve.
