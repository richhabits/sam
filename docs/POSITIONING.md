# SAM — Positioning

The one-sentence answer to *"why does this win?"* — not a feature list. Candidates, then the pick.

## The winner (recommended)

> **SAM is the only AI assistant that's genuinely private, works offline and free out of the box, and
> gets *more useful the more you use it* — because it learns you on your device, not in someone's cloud.**

Why this one: it names the three things no competitor has all of (private + free/offline + compounding
personalization), and the last clause is the unique, uncopyable moat. It's a promise a skeptic can verify
in 60 seconds (pull your wifi, it still works).

## Alternates (by angle)

- **Privacy-first:** "A private AI that runs on your machine and actually does the work — your files,
  memory and keys never leave the device."
- **Does-the-work:** "What Cursor did for coding, SAM does for your whole computer — an AI that takes real
  action, free, on your own machine."
- **Free/local:** "A team of AI agents that lives on your computer and costs ~£0 — free cloud tiers or
  fully offline on Ollama."
- **Anti-subscription:** "The AI assistant you own, not rent. No subscription, no telemetry, no lock-in —
  it gets better the longer you keep it."

## What NOT to lead with

- Tool count / feature lists (commoditized, and invites "ChatGPT has plugins too").
- "Team of agents" alone (impressive demo, but not the retention reason).
- Price alone ("free" reads as "cheap/unserious" without the privacy + compounding-value frame).

## The 3-second version (for a badge / tweet / HN title)

**"Private, local-first AI that learns you — free, offline, yours."**
